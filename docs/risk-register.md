# AI Tiage Risk Register

| Risk Name | Category | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation | Signal of Success |
|-----------|----------|------------|--------|------------|-------------------|
| Incomplete Patient Information | AI-Technical | Medium - During busy periods, patients records may be incomplete before the AI generates a recommendation | High - Missing information can cause the AI to miscalculate the patient's severity and assign an incorrect triage level. | The triage application will prevent AI recommendations until all required clinical fields are completed or a clinician documents a reason for proceeding | Less that 2% of AI assessments are completed with missing required clinical information during audits |
| Clinical Guideline Drift | AI-Technical | Medium - Hospital triage guidelines can change while the AI continues using outdated decision patterns | High - AI recommendations may no longer align with current clinical practices, increasing the risk of incorrect triage decisions. | The AI team will review the model after every guideline change | Updated model is validated and redeployed within 15 days of a guideline revision. |
Workflow Delays During Peak Hours | Operational | Medium - Staff may require additional time to review AI recommendations during high patient volumes. | Medium - Longer assessment times could increase waiting time for patients. | Emergency department managers will monitor average triage times during the pilot. Adjustments and retraining of staff will occur if triage time increases by more than 1 minute | Average triage times remai within one minute. |
| System Downtime During Emergency Operations | Operational | Low - Network failures and power outages may disable the AI system temporarily | High - Staff can lose access to the AI during the outages | A manual triage protocol wil be maintained and tested. Staff will receive refresher training on operating without AI support. | Downtime drills demonstrate uninterrupted clinical operations without AI assistance. |
| Lack of Explainability for AI Recommendations | Ethical | Medium - Clinicians may receive an AI recommendation without understanding the factors that influenced it | High - Staff may struggle to justify triage decisions to patient and other clinicians, which reduces trust and accountability. | Implementation of an explanation feature that would display the key clinical factors that influence each AI recommendation | 90% of clinicians report they can understand and explain the AI recommendations during evaluations. |
Unauthorized Use of Patient's Data | Ethical | Low - Patients' data collected from triage could later be used for research or development of AI model without approval | High - Misuse of patients' information could violate ethical standards and exposed the hospital to legal risks. | All uses of patients' data will require approval from the hospital's ethics committee. Annual audits will verify compliance with data governance policies. | Annual audits confirms 100% of patient's data use has ethics approval and complies with governance policies. |
| Limited Representation of Rural Patient Populations | Equity | Medium - Training data collected primarily from once source mau not adequately represent patients in other healthcare facilities. | High - The AI may perform less accurately for patients whose characteristics differ from the training population. | Additional datasets from different healthcare facilities will be incorporated into the training data. | Perfromace differences between hospitals remain within 5% after validation. |
| Reduced Accuracy for Patients with Multiple Chronic Conditions | Equity | Medium - Patients with several chronic diseases often present with complex or atypical symptoms that may not be sufficiently represented in training data. | High - Lower prediction accuracy could delay identification of high risk patients that require urgent care. | Subgroup analyses will be conducted for patients with multiple chronic conditions. If recall differs by more than 5% addition data will be collected and the model will be retrained | Recall for patients with multiple chronic conditions remain within 5% of the overall model performance. |
